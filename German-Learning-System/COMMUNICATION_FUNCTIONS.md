# Communication Functions

## Purpose

Communication functions are the bridge between grammar knowledge and real-life use.

Learners do not only need to know rules. They need to use German to do things:

- ask for help
- explain a problem
- give reasons
- express opinions
- make suggestions
- tell stories
- agree and disagree
- manage conversations

This document connects the German Learning System's major design layers:

- `VISION.md` defines the promise: turning scattered knowledge into usable communication.
- `LEARNING_MODEL.md` defines the lesson journey from real-life situation to confidence.
- `GRAMMAR_MAP.md` defines the connected grammar system.
- `SENTENCE_ARCHITECTURE_MAP.md` defines how ideas are built and connected in sentences.

Communication functions explain what learners can actually do when these pieces work together.

## Detailed Function Examples

### 1. Asking For Help

#### Function

The learner asks another person for practical support, clarification, or assistance.

#### Why It Matters

Asking for help is one of the earliest confidence-building functions. It allows learners to participate even when they do not have complete language control.

#### CEFR Progression From A1 To C1

- A1: Ask for simple help with a memorized phrase.
- A2: Explain the basic situation and ask for a specific kind of help.
- B1: Describe the problem, ask follow-up questions, and clarify what is needed.
- B2: Ask for support in formal, workplace, institutional, or service contexts.
- C1: Ask diplomatically, precisely, and with appropriate register in complex situations.

#### Sample Phrases By Level

| Level | Sample Phrases |
| --- | --- |
| A1 | Entschuldigung, koennen Sie mir helfen? Ich brauche Hilfe. Ich verstehe das nicht. |
| A2 | Koennen Sie mir bitte kurz helfen? Ich suche die Apotheke. Ich habe ein Problem mit dem Termin. |
| B1 | Ich bin mir nicht sicher, was ich machen soll. Koennten Sie mir erklaeren, wie das funktioniert? |
| B2 | Ich waere Ihnen dankbar, wenn Sie mir bei diesem Anliegen weiterhelfen koennten. |
| C1 | Waere es moeglich, dass Sie mir kurz einordnen, welche Schritte in dieser Situation sinnvoll waeren? |

#### Grammar That Supports It

- Modal verbs: koennen, muessen, sollen
- Polite Konjunktiv II: koennten, wuerden, waere
- Dative pronouns: mir, Ihnen
- Question formation
- Infinitive clauses with zu for more advanced requests

#### Sentence Architecture That Supports It

- Yes-no questions
- W-questions
- Main clauses with polite framing
- Subordinate clauses with wenn
- Infinitive clauses for complex requests

#### Real-Life Scenarios

- Asking a stranger for directions.
- Asking a pharmacist for help.
- Asking a colleague to explain a task.
- Asking an office which form is needed.
- Asking a landlord how to report a repair.

#### Example Learner Goal

I can ask for help politely, explain the basic situation, and understand the next step.

### 2. Explaining A Problem

#### Function

The learner describes what is wrong, what happened, and what they need.

#### Why It Matters

Adult learners often need German most when something is not working: a medical issue, a broken appliance, a missed appointment, a school message, or a workplace misunderstanding.

#### CEFR Progression From A1 To C1

- A1: Name the problem with short phrases.
- A2: Describe the problem with simple details.
- B1: Explain what happened, when it happened, and what is needed.
- B2: Explain causes, consequences, and possible solutions.
- C1: Present complex problems with precision, diplomacy, and documentation.

#### Sample Phrases By Level

| Level | Sample Phrases |
| --- | --- |
| A1 | Ich habe ein Problem. Das funktioniert nicht. Ich bin krank. |
| A2 | Meine Heizung ist kaputt. Ich habe seit gestern Halsschmerzen. Der Termin passt leider nicht. |
| B1 | Ich wollte den Termin wahrnehmen, aber ich konnte nicht kommen, weil mein Kind krank war. |
| B2 | Das Problem besteht seit mehreren Tagen und beeintraechtigt meinen Alltag erheblich. |
| C1 | Aus meiner Sicht liegt das Problem darin, dass die vereinbarte Leistung bisher nicht vollstaendig erbracht wurde. |

#### Grammar That Supports It

- Present tense for current problems
- Perfekt and Prateritum for what happened
- Dative and accusative objects
- weil, dass, wenn, obwohl
- Passive voice for formal problem descriptions

#### Sentence Architecture That Supports It

- Main clauses for facts
- Perfekt verb bracket
- Subordinate clauses for reasons and context
- Connector chains for sequence and consequence
- Formal written sentence architecture at B2-C1

#### Real-Life Scenarios

- Reporting a repair to a landlord.
- Explaining symptoms to a doctor.
- Writing to a school about an absence.
- Contacting customer service.
- Explaining a workplace issue.

#### Example Learner Goal

I can explain a practical problem clearly enough that another person can help me.

### 3. Giving Reasons

#### Function

The learner explains why something is true, why they made a decision, or why something happened.

#### Why It Matters

Giving reasons helps learners move from isolated statements to connected communication. It supports appointments, apologies, opinions, complaints, work conversations, and exam writing.

#### CEFR Progression From A1 To C1

- A1: Give two simple statements side by side.
- A2: Connect an action and a reason with weil.
- B1: Give personal reasons and goals with weil, deshalb, damit.
- B2: Use da, aus diesem Grund, deshalb, and structured justification.
- C1: Express layered reasons, social meaning, and abstract motivation.

#### Sample Phrases By Level

| Level | Sample Phrases |
| --- | --- |
| A1 | Ich lerne Deutsch. Ich wohne in Hamburg. |
| A2 | Ich lerne Deutsch, weil ich in Hamburg wohne. |
| B1 | Ich lerne Deutsch, weil ich mich im Alltag sicherer fuehlen moechte. |
| B2 | Ich lerne Deutsch, da ich beruflich und privat selbststaendiger kommunizieren moechte. |
| C1 | Ich lerne Deutsch nicht nur aus praktischen Gruenden, sondern auch, weil Sprache ein wichtiger Teil gesellschaftlicher Teilhabe ist. |

#### Grammar That Supports It

- Present tense
- Modal verbs
- Subordinate clauses
- Infinitive structures
- Nouns and nominal phrases for abstract reasons

#### Sentence Architecture That Supports It

- Hauptsatz
- Nebensatz
- Verb-final word order
- Sentence adverbs in position 1
- Coordinating and subordinating connector contrast

#### Real-Life Scenarios

- Explaining why an appointment needs to change.
- Giving a reason for learning German.
- Explaining a decision at work.
- Writing an exam response.
- Justifying a request or complaint.

#### Example Learner Goal

I can connect what I say to a clear reason, using the right sentence structure.

### 4. Expressing An Opinion

#### Function

The learner says what they think, feel, prefer, or believe about a topic.

#### Why It Matters

Opinion language helps learners move beyond survival German into social, workplace, exam, and community participation.

#### CEFR Progression From A1 To C1

- A1: Say simple likes and dislikes.
- A2: Give a simple opinion with a reason.
- B1: Express opinions on familiar topics and respond to others.
- B2: Present a structured opinion with examples and counterpoints.
- C1: Express nuanced positions with register, qualification, and rhetorical control.

#### Sample Phrases By Level

| Level | Sample Phrases |
| --- | --- |
| A1 | Ich finde das gut. Das ist interessant. Ich mag Kaffee. |
| A2 | Ich finde den Kurs gut, weil ich viel spreche. |
| B1 | Meiner Meinung nach ist es wichtig, jeden Tag ein bisschen zu ueben. |
| B2 | Aus meiner Sicht ueberwiegen die Vorteile, auch wenn es einige Nachteile gibt. |
| C1 | Ich wuerde die Frage differenzierter betrachten, weil sowohl praktische als auch gesellschaftliche Aspekte eine Rolle spielen. |

#### Grammar That Supports It

- finden + accusative
- weil and dass clauses
- Comparatives and superlatives
- Konjunktiv II for softening
- Nominal phrases for abstract ideas

#### Sentence Architecture That Supports It

- Main clauses for direct opinions
- Subordinate clauses for reasons
- Adverbial connectors for argument flow
- Discourse markers: meiner Meinung nach, aus meiner Sicht, einerseits, andererseits

#### Real-Life Scenarios

- Discussing a class, neighborhood, job, or service.
- Giving feedback.
- Participating in B1/B2 speaking exams.
- Discussing options with friends or coworkers.
- Writing an opinion text.

#### Example Learner Goal

I can state my opinion and support it with at least one clear reason.

### 5. Making A Suggestion

#### Function

The learner proposes an action, plan, solution, or next step.

#### Why It Matters

Suggestions are central to social life, problem solving, teamwork, appointments, and negotiation.

#### CEFR Progression From A1 To C1

- A1: Suggest a simple activity or time.
- A2: Suggest plans using koennen or wollen.
- B1: Make suggestions with reasons and alternatives.
- B2: Recommend a solution in practical or professional contexts.
- C1: Frame suggestions diplomatically and strategically.

#### Sample Phrases By Level

| Level | Sample Phrases |
| --- | --- |
| A1 | Wir trinken Kaffee. Gehen wir? |
| A2 | Wir koennen morgen telefonieren. Vielleicht treffen wir uns um drei. |
| B1 | Ich schlage vor, dass wir zuerst den Termin klaeren. |
| B2 | Es waere sinnvoll, zunaechst die wichtigsten Punkte zu sammeln. |
| C1 | Aus meiner Sicht waere es zielfuehrend, die Optionen zu priorisieren und anschliessend gemeinsam eine Entscheidung zu treffen. |

#### Grammar That Supports It

- Modal verbs: koennen, sollen
- Imperative and suggestion forms
- dass clauses
- Infinitive clauses with zu
- Konjunktiv II for polite proposals

#### Sentence Architecture That Supports It

- Main clauses
- Yes-no questions
- dass subordinate clauses
- Infinitive clauses
- Discourse markers for sequencing: zuerst, danach, anschliessend

#### Real-Life Scenarios

- Planning a meeting with a friend.
- Suggesting a solution to a landlord or service provider.
- Participating in workplace planning.
- Offering advice in a course or family context.

#### Example Learner Goal

I can suggest a practical next step and make it sound appropriate for the situation.

### 6. Agreeing And Disagreeing

#### Function

The learner responds to another person's idea with agreement, partial agreement, or polite disagreement.

#### Why It Matters

Agreement and disagreement help learners participate in real conversation instead of only answering questions.

#### CEFR Progression From A1 To C1

- A1: Agree or disagree with simple phrases.
- A2: Add a short reason.
- B1: Disagree politely and explain why.
- B2: Partly agree, contrast ideas, and respond to counterpoints.
- C1: Manage disagreement with nuance, diplomacy, and rhetorical control.

#### Sample Phrases By Level

| Level | Sample Phrases |
| --- | --- |
| A1 | Ja, das stimmt. Nein, das stimmt nicht. Ich auch. |
| A2 | Ich bin einverstanden, aber ich habe keine Zeit. |
| B1 | Ich verstehe deinen Punkt, aber ich sehe das anders, weil... |
| B2 | Da stimme ich teilweise zu; allerdings sollte man auch beachten, dass... |
| C1 | Dem kann ich grundsaetzlich zustimmen, dennoch wuerde ich den Aspekt der Umsetzbarkeit staerker gewichten. |

#### Grammar That Supports It

- Negation with nicht and kein
- aber, obwohl, trotzdem, allerdings
- dass clauses
- Modal verbs for perspective and advice
- Konjunktiv II for softening disagreement

#### Sentence Architecture That Supports It

- Main clauses
- Coordinating connectors
- Adverbial connectors in position 1
- Subordinate clauses for reasons and concessions
- Discourse markers for stance

#### Real-Life Scenarios

- Discussing plans with friends.
- Responding in a meeting.
- Negotiating household or work decisions.
- Participating in exam discussions.
- Giving feedback.

#### Example Learner Goal

I can agree or disagree without stopping the conversation.

### 7. Telling A Past Event

#### Function

The learner explains something that happened in the past.

#### Why It Matters

Past events appear in daily life constantly: what happened at the doctor, why someone was late, how a trip went, what happened at work, or what someone said.

#### CEFR Progression From A1 To C1

- A1: Name simple past-like context with time words, mostly in present or memorized chunks.
- A2: Use Perfekt to describe common past events.
- B1: Tell connected stories using sequence and contrast.
- B2: Narrate events with cause, consequence, and perspective.
- C1: Report complex events with nuance, register, and implied meaning.

#### Sample Phrases By Level

| Level | Sample Phrases |
| --- | --- |
| A1 | Gestern: Arbeit. Heute: Deutschkurs. |
| A2 | Gestern habe ich einen Termin gemacht. Dann bin ich nach Hause gefahren. |
| B1 | Zuerst habe ich angerufen, aber niemand hat geantwortet. Danach habe ich eine E-Mail geschrieben. |
| B2 | Nachdem ich mehrere Tage gewartet hatte, habe ich mich erneut an den Kundenservice gewandt. |
| C1 | Rueckblickend war entscheidend, dass die Kommunikation zu Beginn unklar war und dadurch mehrere Missverstaendnisse entstanden sind. |

#### Grammar That Supports It

- Perfekt
- Prateritum of sein, haben, modal verbs
- Plusquamperfekt
- Time expressions
- Connectors for sequence and contrast
- Reported speech at higher levels

#### Sentence Architecture That Supports It

- Perfekt verb bracket
- Main clauses in sequence
- Subordinate clauses with nachdem, bevor, als, weil
- Adverbial connectors: zuerst, dann, danach, spaeter

#### Real-Life Scenarios

- Explaining why you missed an appointment.
- Describing a trip.
- Reporting a workplace problem.
- Talking about a doctor's visit.
- Writing a B1 personal letter or email.

#### Example Learner Goal

I can tell someone what happened in a clear order.

### 8. Making An Appointment

#### Function

The learner schedules, confirms, changes, or cancels an appointment.

#### Why It Matters

Appointments are a high-frequency adult-life function across healthcare, work, housing, education, offices, and services.

#### CEFR Progression From A1 To C1

- A1: Ask for a simple appointment time.
- A2: Make, change, or cancel an appointment with a reason.
- B1: Explain availability, constraints, and preferences.
- B2: Handle formal scheduling and negotiate alternatives.
- C1: Manage complex scheduling diplomatically across professional or institutional contexts.

#### Sample Phrases By Level

| Level | Sample Phrases |
| --- | --- |
| A1 | Ich brauche einen Termin. Haben Sie am Montag Zeit? |
| A2 | Ich moechte meinen Termin verschieben, weil ich arbeiten muss. |
| B1 | Der Termin am Dienstag passt mir leider nicht. Waere ein Termin am Freitag moeglich? |
| B2 | Aufgrund eines beruflichen Termins muesste ich unseren Termin leider verschieben. |
| C1 | Ich waere Ihnen dankbar, wenn wir einen alternativen Termin finden koennten, da sich kurzfristig eine terminliche Ueberschneidung ergeben hat. |

#### Grammar That Supports It

- Time expressions
- Modal verbs
- Accusative for einen Termin
- Separable verbs: anrufen, absagen
- Subordinate clauses with weil, da, wenn
- Konjunktiv II for politeness

#### Sentence Architecture That Supports It

- Questions
- Main clauses
- Modal verb brackets
- Subordinate reason clauses
- Formal email structure

#### Real-Life Scenarios

- Calling a doctor's office.
- Booking a service appointment.
- Changing a work meeting.
- Confirming a school appointment.
- Writing to a landlord or government office.

#### Example Learner Goal

I can make or change an appointment and explain my availability.

### 9. Complaining Politely

#### Function

The learner expresses dissatisfaction, explains the issue, and requests a solution while maintaining appropriate tone.

#### Why It Matters

Polite complaints help learners advocate for themselves in shops, housing, services, work, and institutions.

#### CEFR Progression From A1 To C1

- A1: Say something is wrong.
- A2: Explain the problem and ask for help.
- B1: Write or say a simple complaint with details.
- B2: Structure a formal complaint with evidence, expectation, and request.
- C1: Escalate or negotiate a complaint diplomatically and precisely.

#### Sample Phrases By Level

| Level | Sample Phrases |
| --- | --- |
| A1 | Das ist nicht richtig. Das funktioniert nicht. |
| A2 | Ich habe ein Problem mit der Bestellung. Koennen Sie mir bitte helfen? |
| B1 | Leider bin ich mit dem Produkt nicht zufrieden, weil es nach zwei Tagen kaputt war. |
| B2 | Ich moechte Sie bitten, den Fall zu pruefen und mir eine angemessene Loesung anzubieten. |
| C1 | Vor diesem Hintergrund bitte ich um eine zeitnahe Klaerung sowie um eine nachvollziehbare Rueckmeldung zu den weiteren Schritten. |

#### Grammar That Supports It

- Negation
- Perfekt for what happened
- weil, da, obwohl
- Passive voice for formal descriptions
- Konjunktiv II for polite requests
- Formal noun phrases

#### Sentence Architecture That Supports It

- Main clause facts
- Subordinate clauses for reasons
- Adverbial connectors for consequence
- Formal paragraph structure
- Passive or impersonal constructions at B2-C1

#### Real-Life Scenarios

- Returning a broken item.
- Reporting a housing problem.
- Contacting customer service.
- Writing to an institution.
- Resolving a workplace or service issue.

#### Example Learner Goal

I can explain what is wrong and ask for a fair solution politely.

### 10. Reaching Agreement

#### Function

The learner helps a conversation move toward a shared decision, compromise, or next step.

#### Why It Matters

Reaching agreement is essential for social planning, family life, teamwork, housing, work, and community participation.

#### CEFR Progression From A1 To C1

- A1: Confirm a simple plan.
- A2: Choose between options.
- B1: Agree on a plan after discussing preferences.
- B2: Negotiate conditions and summarize decisions.
- C1: Facilitate consensus and articulate tradeoffs.

#### Sample Phrases By Level

| Level | Sample Phrases |
| --- | --- |
| A1 | Ja, das passt. Okay, um drei. |
| A2 | Freitag passt mir besser. Wir koennen uns um vier treffen. |
| B1 | Dann machen wir es so: Ich rufe morgen an, und du schreibst die E-Mail. |
| B2 | Wenn alle einverstanden sind, koennen wir diesen Vorschlag als naechsten Schritt festhalten. |
| C1 | Damit scheinen wir eine Loesung gefunden zu haben, die sowohl praktikabel ist als auch die wichtigsten Bedenken beruecksichtigt. |

#### Grammar That Supports It

- Modal verbs
- Conditional clauses with wenn
- Future meaning with present tense or werden
- Connectors for sequence and result
- Konjunktiv II for diplomatic framing

#### Sentence Architecture That Supports It

- Main clauses for decisions
- wenn clauses for conditions
- Adverbial connectors: dann, deshalb, also
- Discourse markers: zusammenfassend, einerseits, andererseits

#### Real-Life Scenarios

- Planning with friends.
- Dividing tasks at work.
- Resolving a family schedule conflict.
- Negotiating with a landlord or service provider.
- Closing a meeting.

#### Example Learner Goal

I can help turn a discussion into a clear shared next step.

## Example: Giving Reasons

Giving reasons is a model function because it shows how grammar, sentence architecture, and communication grow together.

### Progression

```text
A1:
Ich lerne Deutsch.
Ich wohne in Hamburg.

A2:
Ich lerne Deutsch, weil ich in Hamburg wohne.

B1:
Ich lerne Deutsch, weil ich mich im Alltag sicherer fuehlen moechte.

B2:
Ich lerne Deutsch, da ich beruflich und privat selbststaendiger kommunizieren moechte.

C1:
Ich lerne Deutsch nicht nur aus praktischen Gruenden, sondern auch, weil Sprache ein wichtiger Teil gesellschaftlicher Teilhabe ist.
```

### System Connections

| Element | Role In The Function |
| --- | --- |
| weil | Introduces a reason in a Nebensatz; sends the conjugated verb to the end |
| denn | Connects a reason as a main clause; verb stays in position 2 |
| deshalb | Points from cause to result; often position 1 with verb in position 2 |
| da | Introduces a reason; common in more formal or written contexts |
| aus diesem Grund | Formal result phrase; supports B2-C1 written structure |
| Hauptsatz | Carries the main statement or result |
| Nebensatz | Carries the reason or explanation |
| Verb-final word order | Required after subordinating connectors such as weil and da |

### Product Interpretation

Giving reasons turns simple statements into meaningful communication.

```text
A1: separate facts
A2: fact + reason
B1: reason + personal goal
B2: formal reason + broader independence
C1: practical reason + social meaning + abstract participation
```

| Reaching agreement | Ja, das passt. | Freitag passt besser. | Dann machen wir es so: ... | Wenn alle einverstanden sind, koennen wir... | Damit haben wir eine praktikable Loesung gefunden. | Modals, wenn, future meaning | Conditions, summary markers | Meetings, planning, negotiation |

## Design Implication

Every lesson in the German Learning System should be tagged by communication function.

Function tags make lessons more useful because they reveal the real capability being built. A grammar point should never appear alone. It should be attached to something the learner can do.

### Example: Apotheke Lesson

A lesson about the Apotheke might include:

- asking for help
- describing symptoms
- making a request
- understanding advice
- thanking

Possible system tags:

```text
Real-life situation: Apotheke
Communication functions: asking for help, describing symptoms, making a request
Grammar: modal verbs, accusative objects, dative pronouns
Sentence architecture: yes-no questions, W-questions, main clauses
CEFR level: A1-A2
Confidence milestone: I can ask for basic help at the Apotheke without looking up every word before I leave the house.
```

### Example: TELC B1 Writing Lesson

A lesson about TELC B1 writing might include:

- explaining a problem
- giving reasons
- making suggestions
- apologizing
- asking for information

Possible system tags:

```text
Real-life situation: written exam email
Communication functions: explaining a problem, giving reasons, making a suggestion
Grammar: Perfekt, modal verbs, weil, dass, Konjunktiv II basics
Sentence architecture: main clauses, subordinate clauses, connector contrast
CEFR level: B1
Confidence milestone: I can write a clear email that explains the situation, gives a reason, and asks for a practical next step.
```

## Product Principle

Grammar explains how language works.

Sentence architecture explains how ideas connect.

Communication functions explain what learners can do.

The goal is not to know more German.

The goal is to use German with confidence.
