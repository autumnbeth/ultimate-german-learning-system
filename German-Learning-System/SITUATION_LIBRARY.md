# Situation Library

## 1. Purpose

The Situation Library defines the real-life situations where learners repeatedly apply the same German patterns.

This is not a grammar syllabus. It is the context layer of the Ultimate German Learning System.

```text
FOUNDATION_REFERENCE.md explains how German works.
SITUATION_LIBRARY.md explains where German is used.
LESSON_BLUEPRINT.md explains how each lesson is built.
```

Core principle:

Learners should encounter the same grammar, sentence architecture, and communication functions across many different situations until the patterns become automatic.

Situations are not single lessons. A situation can appear at A1, A2, B1, B2, and C1. The situation deepens over time as the learner gains more language, confidence, and control.

Example: Elternabend

```text
A1:
Introduce yourself and understand simple information.

A2:
Ask simple questions and explain basic concerns.

B1:
Explain a problem, give reasons, and ask for support.

B2:
Discuss progress, compare options, and advocate politely.

C1:
Participate in nuanced educational discussions with confidence.
```

The goal is not to finish a topic. The goal is to become increasingly capable inside it.

## 4. Situation Progression Model

The same situation develops across levels. The learner does not finish the situation once. They revisit it with more language, better grammar control, stronger sentence architecture, and more confidence.

### A. Elternabend

| Level | Learner Capability | Example Language |
| --- | --- | --- |
| A1 | Introduce yourself and understand basic information. | Ich bin Beth. Ich bin die Mutter von Sam. |
| A2 | Ask simple questions and explain basic concerns. | Wann ist der Ausflug? Mein Kind braucht Hilfe in Mathe. |
| B1 | Explain a problem, give reasons, and ask for support. | Ich mache mir Sorgen, weil mein Kind die Hausaufgaben oft nicht versteht. Was koennen wir zu Hause ueben? |
| B2 | Discuss progress, compare options, and advocate politely. | Aus meiner Sicht waere es hilfreich, wenn wir regelmaessiger Rueckmeldung zum Lernstand bekommen koennten. |
| C1 | Participate in nuanced educational discussions with confidence. | Ich wuerde gern gemeinsam klaeren, welche Unterstuetzung langfristig sinnvoll ist und wie wir Schule und Zuhause besser verbinden koennen. |

### B. Apotheke

| Level | Learner Capability | Example Language |
| --- | --- | --- |
| A1 | Ask for help and name one basic need. | Koennen Sie mir helfen? Ich habe Husten. |
| A2 | Describe simple symptoms and ask for medicine. | Ich habe seit gestern Halsschmerzen. Haben Sie etwas dagegen? |
| B1 | Explain what happened, ask follow-up questions, and understand advice. | Ich habe schon Tee getrunken, aber es ist nicht besser geworden. Wie oft soll ich das nehmen? |
| B2 | Compare options and explain constraints politely. | Ich suche etwas, das nicht muede macht, weil ich heute noch arbeiten muss. |
| C1 | Discuss health needs with nuance and confidence. | Ich wuerde gern eine Option waehlen, die gut vertraeglich ist und sich mit meinen anderen Medikamenten vereinbaren laesst. |

### C. Meeting A New Team

| Level | Learner Capability | Example Language |
| --- | --- | --- |
| A1 | Say your name, origin, and simple personal information. | Ich heisse Beth. Ich komme aus den USA. |
| A2 | Say your role and basic work information. | Ich arbeite als UX Designerin. Ich arbeite gern im Team. |
| B1 | Describe experience and current responsibilities. | Ich bin seit mehreren Jahren im Bereich UX taetig und arbeite vor allem an Produktkonzepten. |
| B2 | Explain professional focus and collaboration style. | Mein Schwerpunkt liegt auf Produktstrategie und Nutzerforschung, deshalb arbeite ich eng mit Produktteams zusammen. |
| C1 | Present nuanced professional identity and contribution. | Ich leite komplexe Designinitiativen und verbinde Nutzerforschung, Produktstrategie und teamuebergreifende Abstimmung. |

### D. TELC B1 Writing Email

| Level | Learner Capability | Example Language |
| --- | --- | --- |
| A1 | Write basic personal information and simple needs. | Ich heisse Beth. Ich brauche einen Termin. |
| A2 | Write a short message with a simple reason. | Ich kann morgen nicht kommen, weil ich arbeiten muss. |
| B1 | Write a structured email explaining a problem and asking for a solution. | Leider kann ich den Termin nicht wahrnehmen, weil mein Kind krank ist. Koennten wir bitte einen neuen Termin vereinbaren? |
| B2 | Write a clear formal message with context, reason, and request. | Aufgrund einer kurzfristigen familiaeren Situation muss ich den Termin leider verschieben und bitte Sie um einen alternativen Vorschlag. |
| C1 | Write a polished formal email with nuance and appropriate register. | Ich waere Ihnen dankbar, wenn wir angesichts der kurzfristigen Situation eine passende Alternative finden koennten. Fuer Ihre Flexibilitaet danke ich Ihnen im Voraus. |

## 6. Repetition Model

The same patterns should appear across many topics. A learner should not meet a pattern once, "finish" it, and move on. They should encounter it repeatedly until it becomes available in real communication.

Example: Giving reasons appears in:

- Apotheke
- School emails
- Elternabend
- Work meetings
- Travel problems
- TELC writing
- Complaints
- Invitations
- Doctor appointments
- Housing repairs

This repetition helps learners own the pattern instead of learning it once and forgetting it.

### Pattern Reuse Examples

```text
Giving reasons:
weil, denn, deshalb, da

Situations:
missed appointment, school absence, complaint, work update, travel delay

Outcome:
The learner can explain why something happened or why they need something.
```

```text
Questions:
yes-no questions, W-questions, repair questions

Situations:
Apotheke, train station, teacher meeting, first day at work, hotel check-in

Outcome:
The learner can get information and keep communication alive.
```

```text
Perfekt:
haben/sein + Partizip II

Situations:
weekend stories, doctor visits, work updates, reporting repairs, TELC writing

Outcome:
The learner can say what happened.
```

```text
Modal verbs:
koennen, muessen, sollen, moechte

Situations:
asking for help, making requests, giving advice, planning, workplace tasks

Outcome:
The learner can express ability, need, obligation, preference, and suggestions.
```

### Product Rule

Every high-value pattern should appear across at least ten situations over time.

The learner should experience the pattern in:

- spoken interaction
- writing tasks
- retrieval practice
- real-world challenges
- different levels of formality
- different emotional stakes
- different CEFR levels

## 7. Product Principle

The Foundation Reference explains the language system.

The Situation Library gives learners places to use the system.

Lessons create repeated, supported practice inside those situations.

The goal is not to finish a topic.

The goal is to meet the same patterns in enough real-life contexts that German begins to feel usable.
