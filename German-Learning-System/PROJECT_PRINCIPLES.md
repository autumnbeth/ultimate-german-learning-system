# Project Principles

## Situation-First Learning

Traditional language courses organize content into chapters.

This system organizes content around real-life situations.

Instead of asking:

"What chapter are you on?"

We ask:

"What situation are you trying to navigate?"

Examples:

* Apotheke
* Elternabend
* Bürgeramt
* Vorstellungsgespräch
* Wohnungssuche

The goal is to help learners communicate in situations they actually encounter.

---

## Foundations Before Situations

Situations are built on a common set of language foundations.

FOUNDATION_REFERENCE
↓
COMMUNICATION_FUNCTIONS
↓
SITUATIONS
↓
DIALOGUES
↓
REAL LIFE

The same language patterns appear repeatedly across many situations.

Learners study the foundations once and apply them everywhere.

---

## Communication Over Grammar

Grammar is important.

However, grammar is introduced as a tool for communication rather than as an end goal.

Example:

Giving Reasons
↓
weil
deshalb
denn

These patterns appear naturally across many situations:

* Apotheke
* Schule
* Arbeit
* Behörden
* Wohnen

The focus is communication first and grammar in context.

---

## Learning Mode and Reference Mode

The system supports two ways of use.

### Learning Mode

Learners progress through foundations, communication functions, and situations.

### Reference Mode

Learners can jump directly to the situation they need.

Example:

"I have an appointment at the Bürgeramt tomorrow."

The learner can immediately access:

* useful phrases
* communication functions
* dialogue examples
* writing examples
* real-world challenges

without completing previous chapters.

---

## Real Life Before Vocabulary Lists

Vocabulary is learned through situations.

Avoid organizing content primarily as:

* Family Vocabulary
* Health Vocabulary
* Travel Vocabulary

Prefer:

* Elternabend
* Apotheke
* Hotel Check-In
* Arzttermin

Vocabulary should emerge naturally from meaningful context.

---

## Every Situation Should Include

* Overview
* Communication Functions
* Key Language Patterns
* Vocabulary in Context
* Practice Dialogues
* Writing Task
* Real-Life Challenge
* Related Situations

---

## Practical Language for Real Life

The purpose of the system is not simply to teach German.

The purpose is to help learners successfully navigate real-life situations in German.
