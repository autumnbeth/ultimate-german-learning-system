# Curriculum

## Overview

The curriculum is organized around CEFR levels and practical life topics. Each level should help learners communicate in real situations while gradually expanding grammar, vocabulary, listening, speaking, reading, and writing.

## Levels

- A1: Survival communication, basic sentence patterns, introductions, everyday needs.
- A2: Expanded daily-life communication, past events, preferences, appointments, simple explanations.
- B1: Independent communication, opinions, plans, problems, stories, workplace and community situations.
- B2: Nuanced communication, argumentation, detailed explanations, professional and academic contexts.
- C1: Fluent, flexible communication, complex texts, precise expression, cultural and professional sophistication.

## Topic Areas

- Travel
- Health
- Shopping
- Housing
- Work
- Family
- Food
- Education
- Daily life

## Lesson Pattern

Each lesson should include:

- A real-life communication goal.
- Useful vocabulary in context.
- One focused grammar or language pattern.
- Listening or reading input.
- Guided speaking practice.
- Writing or reflection practice.
- Review and transfer task.
