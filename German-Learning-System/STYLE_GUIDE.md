# Style Guide

## Voice

Clear, practical, encouraging, and adult.

The tone should make learners feel capable without pretending German is effortless. Explain directly, use natural examples, and avoid unnecessary academic language.

## Lesson Style

- Start with a real situation.
- Use short explanations followed by examples.
- Prefer useful phrases over isolated word lists.
- Show patterns learners can reuse.
- Include small speaking tasks early.
- End with a practical review or real-world transfer.

## Formatting

- Use descriptive headings.
- Keep paragraphs short.
- Use tables for vocabulary when helpful.
- In vocabulary tables, every German noun must include gender and plural form.
- Use learner-readable noun formatting: `der Termin, die Termine`, `die Adresse, die Adressen`, `das Formular, die Formulare`.
- Mark plural-only or mostly singular nouns clearly, such as `die Schmerzen (Plural)` or `das Wasser, meist Singular`.
- Use bullet lists for steps, examples, and practice prompts.
- Keep German examples accurate and natural.

## Learner Experience

Learners should feel:

- Grounded.
- Oriented.
- Able to start speaking before they are perfect.
- Clear about what they are practicing and why.
- Encouraged by visible progress.
